# list-abm-account-selection

Use this skill to systematically identify the exact handful of Enterprise Target
Accounts that can actually move the needle on your revenue targets.

## Revenue Reverse-Engineering

Start with the math, not the list.
1. **The Goal:** E.g., $1,000,000 in new ARR.
2. **The ACV:** E.g., Average Deal Size = $100,000. (You need 10 wins).
3. **The Win Rate:** E.g., 20% Close Rate from Stage 2. (You need 50 SQLs).
4. **The SQL Rate:** E.g., 5% of ABM targeted accounts turn into SQLs.
5. **The TAM Requirement:** You must select exactly 1,000 highly qualified accounts for this ABM tier to mathematically achieve the goal.

## The Selection Committee

ABM account lists are not built in a vacuum by marketing. They require a documented agreement between:
- **Sales Leadership:** Verifying the AE has the capacity to work the account deeply.
- **Marketing/RevOps:** Verifying they can provide adequate top-of-funnel air cover (ads) and data enrichment for the account.

## Sizing the Buying Committee

For Tier 1 ABM accounts (e.g., Fortune 500), you must map significantly deeper than standard SMB outreach.
- **Standard SMB:** Target 2-3 contacts (CEO, VP).
- **Mid-Market:** Target 5-7 contacts across 2 departments.
- **Enterprise ABM:** Target 15-25 contacts. Map the entire organizational chart using LinkedIn Sales Navigator. Find the ultimate budget holder, the direct managers, the technical evaluators, and the end-users.

## "Disqualifying" an ABM Account Before Launch

Drop an account from the Tier 1 ABM list *before* you launch if:
- They just signed a multi-year deal with your primary competitor (verified via technographics).
- They lack the minimum user count required to make the ACV mathematical model work.
- The target buying committee is entirely hidden (no LinkedIn presence, no discoverable emails).

## Output Contract
When advising on ABM selection, provide:
- The reverse-engineered math model calculating how many accounts they need on their list.
- A template for the buying committee map (roles that must be identified per account).
- The strict disqualification criteria for their campaign.
