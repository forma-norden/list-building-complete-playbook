# list-icp-definition

Use this skill to help users define their Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) using
a strict 3-layer scoring methodology. Vague ICPs ("B2B SaaS companies") lead to failed campaigns.

## The 3-Layer ICP Model

You cannot build a list until you define all three layers.

### Layer 1: Firmographics (The Baseline)
The strict minimum requirements to even be considered a prospect.
- **Components:** Employee Headcount, Annual Revenue, Industry/Vertical, Geography.
- **Example:** "B2B SaaS companies, 50-250 employees, >$5M ARR, headquartered in North America."

### Layer 2: Technographics (The Environment)
The tools they use, which dictate their internal processes and bottlenecks.
- **Components:** CRM, Marketing Automation, Hosting Provider, Payment Gateway.
- **Example:** "Must be using Salesforce and Marketo. Exclude Hubspot users."

### Layer 3: Behavioral/Situational (The Catalyst/Trigger)
The events that make them acutely aware of the problem you solve.
- **Components:** Recent funding, key executive hires, specific job postings (e.g., hiring SDRs), negative reviews on G2.
- **Example:** "Recently raised Series A AND is actively hiring SDRs."

## Defining the "Anti-ICP"

It is equally important to define who you will *not* sell to. This prevents SDRs from wasting time on deals that will never close.
- **Example Anti-ICP:** "Companies under 10 employees (will churn), Companies using legacy on-premise servers (too hard to integrate), E-commerce companies (wrong business model)."

## Output Contract
When asked to help define an ICP, provide:
- A structured breakdown of the 3-Layer Model tailored to their product.
- A clearly defined Anti-ICP constraint.
- Search parameters to use in tools like Apollo or Sales Navigator based on the definition.
